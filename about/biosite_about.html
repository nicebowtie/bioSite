A Life of Music and More...

Sarah Whittemore is a professional vocalist based in Central Florida. Originally from Greensboro, North Carolina, Sarah has called Florida home for the last 16 years. You can most often find Sarah singing at Walt Disney World with the Voices of Liberty or with her a capella group, Voctave. Sarah is also a gifted solo artist, whether crooning with an intimate jazz trio or belting in front of a symphony orchestra. She’s even performed at the hallowed Carnegie Hall. In addition to her live performances, Sarah is an accomplished session singer, recording for dozens of artists and publishing houses. Sarah also released her first solo EP titled “Love Song” in February 2021 where it debuted at #1 on the iTunes jazz charts.

For the uninitiated, how would you describe Sarah’s sound? When asked about her musical influences, the singer had this to say. “I would have to say Ella Fitzgerald, Eva Cassidy, Bjork, Celine Dion, she’s definitely an influence.” If you’ve had the chance to hear Sarah, you’ll recognize those influences right away. As an up and coming vocalist she would spend hours listening to her favorite singers, attempting to match their tone and vocal stylings.

When Sarah isn’t serenading audiences with her silky vocals, she likes to relax by immersing herself in her two favorite hobbies, cooking and Pokemon Go.
